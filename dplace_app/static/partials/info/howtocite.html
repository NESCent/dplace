How to cite

Research that uses data from D-PLACE should cite the following paper:

Kathryn R. Kirby, Russell D. Gray, Simon J. Greenhill, Fiona M. Jordan, Stephanie Gomes-Ng, Hans-Jörg Bibiko, Damián E. Blasi, Carlos A. Botero, Claire Bowern, Carol R. Ember, Dan Leehr, Bobbi S. Low, Joe McCarter, William Divale, and Michael C. Gavin. (2016). D-PLACE: A Global Database of Cultural, Linguistic and Environmental Diversity. PLoS ONE, 11(7): e0158391. doi:10.1371/journal.pone.0158391.

Short version:

Kirby, K.R., Gray, R. D., Greenhill, S. J., Jordan, F. M., Gomes-Ng, S., Bibiko, H-J, et al. (2016). D-PLACE: A Global Database of Cultural, Linguistic and Environmental Diversity. PLoS ONE, 11(7): e0158391. doi:10.1371/journal.pone.0158391.

Users should also cite the original source(s) of the data (see References).

Research using cultural data from the Binford Hunter-Gatherer dataset should cite Binford (2001) and Binford and Johnson (2006).

Research using cultural data from the Ethnographic Atlas dataset should cite, at a minimum, Murdock (1962-1971) and Gray (1999). Any sample that includes societies added to the Ethnographic Atlas by Barry (1980), Korotayev et al. (2004), or Bondarenko et al. (2005) should also cite these three sources.

The reference list citation for Kirby et al. should include the date that data were accessed and the URL for D-PLACE (https://d-place.org), in addition to the full references as above.
